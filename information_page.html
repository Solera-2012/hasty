Commercial Sexual Exploitation of Children:
The Stats

Understanding of the extent of Commercial Sexual Exploitation of Children (CSEC) is severly limited due to a number of factors. More comprehensive studies with both adult and minor individuals represented, as well as a larger sample size, should be conducted to further clarify this point.

CSEC is not uniformly tracked across the US.

Few studies examine this problem, and most are limited by small or skewed sample size.
It is often reported that the average age of entry into sex work is 12-14 years of age.
In some jurisdictions, individuals under the age of 18 are charged with prostitution, even if they are under the legal age of consent in that jurisdiction. As a result, they are often classified as offenders rather than victims.
Top 10 Risk Factors Reported* by Survivors
  1. Poverty
  2. Child Abuse/Neglect
  3. Homelessness
  4. Substance Use
  5. Runaway
  6. Sexual Abuse/Assault
  7. Domestic Violence
  8. Youth Involved with the Child Welfare and/or Juvenile Justice System
  9. Mental Health Concerns
  10. LGBTQ
*reported to NHTRC, BeFree, and Polaris
Demographics of survivors
  • 44% of sex trafficking survivors reported being 17 or younger when they began
  • 95% of survivors identify as female


  • 35% of survivors report being recruited through a romantic situation or marriage proposal
